Orbital Period: The Sun doesn't orbit another object, but the solar system orbits the center of the Milky Way, which takes about 225-250 million years.

The Sun is the star at the center of the solar system, and it provides the energy needed to sustain life on Earth. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, with nuclear fusion reactions occurring at its core that produce light and heat.

Fun Fact: The Sun is so large that about 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it, and it accounts for about 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
